Critical Equity

Nvidia delivers: $81.6B revenue, $1.87 EPS, and a $89–93B Q2 guide — "demand has gone parabolic"

Nvidia reported Q1 revenue of $81.6B versus the $79.2B Street, EPS of $1.87 against $1.77 consensus, with the data-center line at $75.2B. The bigger headline was forward guidance: $89.1B–$92.8B for Q2 against ~$87B expected. CEO Jensen Huang called demand "parabolic" on the call, citing agentic-AI workloads. Shares dipped ~2% in the immediate after-hours print before recovering as the guidance landed.

Market Impact
The single biggest fundamental reset for the AI capex thesis since the March drawdown. Networking sub-segment near $15B de-risks the Blackwell ramp; Asian semi names rallied 2–4% overnight. Megacap leadership returns to the bid.
Watch: NVDA · SMH · QQQ · AVGO · TSM
Source: Al Jazeera · Nvidia record profit

Critical Commodities Macro

Hormuz calming — vessel counts ticking off the floor as Pakistan-mediated talks advance

Shipping traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, which had collapsed to roughly 5% of pre-conflict levels after Iran's February closure, is showing the first credible recovery — Brent has slipped to ~$106 from $108 and tanker tracking is picking up. Trump paused the Navy escort mission (Project Freedom) on May 6 citing "great progress" in Pakistan-mediated talks; Tehran said yesterday it will not seek control of the strait if a deal lands.

Market Impact
Every dollar off crude shaves breakevens and pulls the long end with it — yesterday's 5 bp rally in the 30Y was the bond market voting on this. Reversal headlines remain the single biggest left-tail risk.
Watch: BZ=F · CL=F · XLE · TLT · ZN
Source: Al Jazeera · Iran war updates

Walmart at the bell — Street looks for $174.6B revenue, $0.66 EPS, and the cleanest read on the US consumer

Walmart prints before the open with consensus at $174.57B revenue (+5.4% y/y) and $0.66 EPS (+7.9% y/y). With Target's +5.6% comp yesterday and Home Depot's transactions −1.3%, Walmart is the swing vote on whether trade-down into grocery-led retailers is supporting the broader consumer print. Market Impact
A clean comp + guide raise locks in the consumer-staples bid and helps offset elevated rates pressure on cyclicals. Any margin softness from tariff passthrough is the bearish surprise — XRT and DG are the cleanest reads.

Critical Credit · Rates Macro

Long bonds ease — 30Y backs off 5.19% but the term-premium story is intact

Yesterday's risk-on tone delivered a long-overdue bid for duration: the 30Y closed near 5.11% after touching an 18-year intraday high of 5.19% the day before; the 10Y sits at 4.61%, down ~4 bp on the session. The MOVE index remains elevated near 85 — calm bond markets historically sit sub-80 — and the 2s/10s spread is the steepest since mid-2022.

Market Impact
A relief rally, not a regime change. Until the next Treasury auction prints with normal demand and Fed succession is resolved, duration remains a tactical trade, not a structural allocation.
Watch: TLT · IEF · ^TNX · ^TYX · MOVE
Source: Federal Reserve · H.15 Selected Interest Rates

Target clean beat — comp sales +5.6%, EPS $1.71, raises full-year sales guide

Target delivered Q1 revenue of $25.44B (+6.7% y/y) against $24.66B Street and adjusted EPS of $1.71 versus $1.47 expected. Same-store sales rose 5.6% — the first positive comp in five quarters — and digital comps grew 8.9%. Market Impact
Sharp pivot from yesterday's HD fade — discretionary is bifurcating by execution, not income. Sets the bar for WMT today and for Costco/Best Buy next week.

Watch Commodities Macro

UAE departure leaves OPEC's spare capacity at 2.5 Mb/d — buffer thinnest in a decade

EIA's latest STEO bakes in the UAE's May 1 departure from OPEC: projected 2027 cartel spare capacity falls to 2.5 Mb/d from a prior 3.8 Mb/d. Iraq, Saudi, Kuwait, the UAE, Qatar and Bahrain together shut in roughly 10.5 Mb/d in April; Q2 global inventories are projected to draw ~8.5 Mb/d. Even with Hormuz easing, the structural buffer is gone.

Market Impact
Any subsequent incident — tanker, refinery, pipeline — prices instantly into the long end. WTI options skew is richer than any point in 2022; energy equities still discount the bear case.
Watch: USO · OIH · XOP · CVX · XOM
Source: EIA · Short-Term Energy Outlook

Watch Macro · Policy

Fed in transition — Powell stepped down as chair May 15; April dissent count was the deepest since 1992

Powell's term as chair ended May 15; he remains a governor through 2028 citing legal pressures on Fed independence. The April FOMC's 8–4 hold at 3.50–3.75% was the most dissents in three decades: Stephen Miran wanted a 25 bp cut; Hammack, Kashkari and Logan opposed any easing bias. June pricing is whip-sawing between hold and cut.

Market Impact
Leadership ambiguity is itself a term-premium story — every "Fed independence" headline lifts the back end. Front-end pricing remains hostage to whoever signals next from Eccles.
Watch: SHY · 2YY · DXY · FF futures
