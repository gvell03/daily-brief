CriticalCommoditiesMacroFX

Brent buckles 4.3% as Hormuz transits restart on tentative US–Iran framework, then Tehran threatens reclosure

Brent for August settled −4.34% at $71.99 and WTI at $69.23 (−3.74%) after Persian Gulf throughput recovered to roughly 75% of pre-war levels. But Iran's IRGC reasserted Strait closure citing Israeli strikes in Lebanon as an MOU breach, and Iraq is publicly threatening a quota exit unless OPEC accommodates lost-war revenue. The bid–offer in oil this morning is being set by a single press release in Tehran.

Market Impact

A genuine de-escalation reopens disinflationary space for the Fed and lifts cyclicals and EM. A re-escalation puts $90 Brent back on the screen inside 48 hours and forces a hawkish-stagflation repricing across rates, FX and credit.

CriticalMacroRates

May PCE prints +4.1% — hottest since April 2023 — yet consumer spending barely flinches

Headline PCE accelerated to 4.1% YoY from 3.8% in April, the firmest reading since the early-2023 cycle. Personal consumption stayed solid despite the squeeze, reinforcing the Warsh-era message that rate cuts are not the base case. 9 of 18 FOMC participants now project a hike before year-end; the dot-grid pushed any easing into 2027–28.

Market Impact

Sticky services inflation plus a resilient consumer kills the dovish convexity in front-end rates. Real-yield-sensitive duration proxies — long bonds, gold, growth multiples — face an asymmetric cap until shelter and core services soften.

Watch: 2Y · 5Y · TLT · XLU · QQQ · GLD

Source · Quartz / BEA
03
CriticalEquity

AI-capex anxiety drags Nasdaq to a fifth losing session as OpenAI reportedly delays IPO into 2027

The Nasdaq composite fell 0.24% Friday for a fifth-straight down day, with Western Digital −10.5% leading hardware lower as investors stress-tested data-center capex math. Apple and Microsoft slipped after announcing iPhone and Xbox price hikes tied to memory inflation, while The New York Times reported OpenAI is leaning toward postponing its public debut until 2027.

Market Impact

The capex-to-return ratio is now the dominant megacap KPI. A pause in hyperscaler spend would deflate semis and power names; a re-acceleration revives the trade. Either way the cycle's beta is no longer "AI vs not-AI" but "AI payback within 24 months vs not."

Ukraine launches one of its largest drone barrages of the war as Crimea logistics collapse

Crimea suspended retail fuel sales as Ukrainian SBU operations cut bridges, depots and air defenses; Zelenskyy publicly ordered a 40-day influence operation to compel Moscow to the table.

European gas and refined-product cracks remain twitchy to Russian midstream losses. Defense primes catch a bid; EU peripheral spreads stay anchored on the assumption that the war's end-state, not its escalation, is now the market's working hypothesis.

WatchCrypto

Bitcoin breaks $60k as spot-ETF redemptions hit $692M, the largest single-day outflow since May

BTC traded to an intraday $58,189, the lowest print since September 2024. US spot Bitcoin ETFs shed ~$692M on Thursday; year-over-year ETF holdings growth has stalled to "basically zero." Ethereum fell up to 5.4% and Solana 6.3%, suggesting macro risk-off rather than crypto-specific stress.

Market Impact

Crypto's marginal buyer is now structurally tied to real rates and a hawkish Fed. A break of $55k brings forced-flow risk into view for treasury-loaded equities; a hold here keeps the door open to a tactical squeeze on any soft macro print.

WatchEquity

Micron blowout salvages the chip narrative even as Western Digital craters 10%

Micron's FQ3 print crushed estimates on AI-memory pricing, validating the HBM cycle. The market's reaction was bifurcated: hardware peers without HBM exposure (Western Digital −10.5%) were faded hard while AI-adjacent foundry and design names held. The "AI haves vs have-nots" tape is sharpening.

Market Impact

HBM scarcity is no longer a thesis — it's a P&L. Expect a re-rating in pure-play memory and a continued de-rating in commoditized storage. Pricing-power screens beat capex-leverage screens until the next FOMC.

HY OAS holds at 276bps — historically tight despite war, sticky inflation and a hawkish Fed

ICE BofA US High Yield OAS finished the week at 2.76%, with the June composite running near 2.63%. Spreads are well inside the 5-year average despite a Mideast war and a hawkish Fed pivot — the market is pricing default tolerance, not default avoidance.

WatchPolicy

Warsh-era FOMC holds at 3.50–3.75% and erases its 2026 cut, telegraphing two hikes are now in play

Chair Kevin Warsh's first FOMC voted 12–0 to hold and stripped easing language from the statement. The dot-plot erased the prior single 2026 cut; six members projected two hikes by year-end. Powell remains a voting Governor — the signal is less about dissent and more about a regime change at the top.

Market Impact

Front-end rates are now the carry trade of the cycle. Steepeners work if the long end follows term-premium logic; flatteners work if Warsh actually hikes. The dollar's downside is capped until the rest of the world catches up.

Watch: SOFR · 2Y · DXY · USDJPY · EUR

WatchCommodities

Iraq threatens OPEC exit unless quota is raised, opening the cartel's second wound after UAE departure

Iraq, OPEC's second-largest producer, is publicly seeking a higher quota to recoup wartime losses and has threatened to leave if demands are not met. The cartel just absorbed the UAE's May exit; a second high-quality producer walking would functionally end OPEC's residual supply discipline.

Market Impact

An Iraq exit would re-anchor the marginal barrel toward $55–60 Brent and pressure E&P capex. Refiners get a near-term margin tailwind; sovereigns dependent on $80+ Brent (Russia, Iran, Saudi) face renewed fiscal stress.
