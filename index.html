Global memory chip rout drags Nasdaq down 2.2% as Samsung, SK Hynix and Micron collapse

Asia's session was dubbed “Black Tuesday” after the Kospi fell 10% intraday — its biggest one-day drop on record — with Samsung −12.1%, SK Hynix −14.2%, and US-listed Micron −11.4% to $1,074.60. The selloff travelled to New York: Nvidia −3.2% to $201.97 and Nasdaq −2.21% as concerns over debt-funded AI capex and a more hawkish Fed crystallised.

Brent breaks below $80 as Treasury issues 60-day Iran license and Hormuz traffic resumes

Brent closed at $77.20, the lowest in nearly three months, after the US Treasury issued a 60-day general license authorising Iranian crude transactions following the June 17 US–Iran memorandum of understanding. Commercial tanker traffic through Hormuz has surged from a near-standstill, with 18 vessels transiting in the first 20 hours after signing.

Market Impact

The unwind of the conflict premium has clipped roughly $12 off Brent versus mid-May highs and is the single biggest disinflation impulse into the July CPI print. Energy equities lagging spot suggests buyside is sceptical the truce holds the full 60 days.

Warsh-led Fed holds at 3.50–3.75%, strips dovish bias and lifts dot to 3.8% for year-end

In Kevin Warsh's first FOMC, members voted 12–0 to hold but the statement dropped its bias toward future cuts. The new median dot for end-2026 rose to 3.80% from 3.4% in March — nine of 18 participants now see at least one hike, eight see no change, one sees a cut. The dispersion is the widest since 2022.

Market Impact

The hawkish drift was masked on Tuesday by oil's decline and the chip selloff bid into duration. Front-end pricing has erased two of three cuts previously expected by Jan-27 SOFR. Curve is at +44bps 2s10s and steepening on hike-then-pause logic.

ECB hikes 25bps to 2.25% deposit rate, diverging from a paralysed Fed on Hormuz inflation

The Governing Council lifted the deposit facility to 2.25%, MRO to 2.40% and marginal lending to 2.65%, effective June 17. The statement cited Middle East inflation pass-through as “robust across a range of scenarios.” This is the first ECB hike since 2023 and breaks a year-long pause.

Market Impact

EUR/USD has lifted toward 1.10 but the rally is capped by a DXY firming on Warsh's hawkish hold. Bund-Treasury spread tightening; periphery (BTPs) widened modestly. A Fed-ECB policy gap of ~150bps is the narrowest in three years.
