Ceasefire Bets Drive Oil Off Session Highs as Hormuz Remains Effectively Shuttered
Brent crude rose +1.89% to $104.52/bbl on the cash close but was trading closer to $102.58 in Thursday's pre-market session as investors increasingly bet on a resolution to the US-Iran conflict. The Strait of Hormuz has been effectively closed since February 28, 2026, when the US and Israel launched air operations against Iran and Iranian forces declared the strait "closed" to commercial traffic. The IEA has characterised this as the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market. A conditional ceasefire is in place, but near-zero shipping has transited the strait, and talks remain fragile.
Market Impact

EM importers (India, Turkey) facing severe current account strain. A genuine reopening of Hormuz would unleash a sharp deflationary oil shock.
