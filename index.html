Brent slides to three-month low as Washington grants Iran a 60-day oil licence and Hormuz keeps flowing

Brent fell roughly 2.4% to $77.20 and WTI to $73.67 after the US extended a 60-day window for Iran to ship into international markets and CENTCOM confirmed 55 merchant ships transited Hormuz on June 20 carrying 17M+ barrels. Tehran's declared closure has not been enforced, and a full reopening could release roughly 80M barrels into the market against still-soft demand.

Market ImpactEnergy equities under pressure; airlines and consumer cyclicals catch a bid; breakeven inflation softens, easing the bear-steepener that ran post-FOMC. Watch: XLE, JETS, USO, BNO, TIPS, 5y5y BE.
